SONY

History

Sony, a global tech giant, was founded in 1946 by Masaru Ibuka and Akio Morita in Tokyo, Japan. Originally named Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo K.K., it evolved into Sony Corporation. Sony pioneered innovations, creating iconic products like the transistor radio, Walkman, and PlayStation. The company expanded into entertainment with acquisitions of CBS Records and Columbia Pictures. Sony consistently developed proprietary standards, notably the compact disc and Blu-ray. Renowned for intellectual property, it consistently ranks among the world's top patent applicants. Sony's journey reflects a commitment to technological excellence and innovative consumer electronics.
